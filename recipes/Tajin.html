Tajine

Description

Tajine, also known as tagine, refers both to the rich Moroccan stews and the cooking vessel used to make them. When referring
to the cooking vessel, it is a round and shallow, clay or ceramic casserole with a tall, pointy, conical lid.

The same lid fits into the base, so the steam condenses on its interior and drops back into the stew without any waste of flavors
and moisture. The pots are ideal for slow cooking over embers or on a stove, resulting in buttery, tender vegetables and meat
that falls off the bone.
